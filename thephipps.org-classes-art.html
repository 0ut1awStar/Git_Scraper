Art Classes & Studios

Explore your creativity with the Phipps! Deepen your skills or learn something new with an ongoing class, a one-day workshop, or a studio experience! We offer art classes and studios for all ages and skill levels in pottery, drawing, and more. Each art experience is an opportunity to realize your vision.

Visual Arts Classes 2025

Art for Kids: Afterschool Art

6 Wednesdays, 4:30-6 p.m.
Spring Session: 4/2-5/7
Ages 8-12
Fee: $127
Instructor: Petra Guenther-Johnson

Come get creative in our afterschool art class with teaching artist Petra Guenther-Johnson! Perfect for kids who love to draw, paint, and make things, this class is all about having fun and expressing yourself. Experiment with different materials and techniques, develop your skills as an artist, and explore your imagination in this welcoming weekly class. All materials included.

Class is limited to 12.

Pottery Night Out

Friday, April 4, 6:30-8:30 p.m.

Saturday, May 10, 6:30-8:30 p.m.
Ages 18+
Fee: $49 per person, including concessions beverage voucher

Have you ever wanted to learn how to throw on the potter’s wheel? This one-night workshop is for you! It will be a fun night out, so bring your partner, family or group of friends. Participants will receive a wheel throwing demonstration and time to throw on the wheel with instructor guidance. Students will choose a glaze color, and instructors will glaze and fire pieces to be ready for pick up approximately 2 weeks after the class date. Beverage voucher may only be redeemed that evening at Phipps concessions.

Class size is limited to 9.

Register

Pottery Night Out: Spring Planter Pot

Saturday, April 26, 6:30-8:30 p.m.
Ages 18+
Fee: $49 per person, including concessions beverage voucher

Bring on spring with this one-night workshop for making your own planter pot! Learn basic hand-building techniques such as pinching, coiling, and clay slab-construction as you design, build and decorate your projects. Bring your partner, family, or group of friends. Instructors will glaze and fire pieces to be ready for pick up approximately 2-3 weeks after the class date. Beverage voucher may only be redeemed that evening at Phipps concessions.

Class size is limited to 12.

Creative Together: Adult/Child Clay Garden Gnomes

Saturday, March 29, 10 a.m. – 12 p.m.
Saturday, March 29, 1 – 3 p.m. [WAITLIST]
Adult with child 5+
Fee: $32 per person – adult and child MUST register as separate students

Kids (ages 5+) and their adults will make their own hand-built garden gnomes using slab, pinch, and coil techniques. Design, create and decorate your projects and make memories together along the way! The instructor will coat with a clear glaze and kiln fire the projects which will be available for pick up 2 weeks after the class.

Class size is limited to 14.

Pottery Open Studio

March 31-May 23 [WAITLIST]
M, W, F, 10:00 a.m.-8:00 p.m.
T, Th, 10:00 a.m.-5:00 p.m.
Sat, 10:00 a.m.-8:00 p.m.
Age 18+

Open studio gives experienced students the freedom to practice their wheel throwing and hand building skills independently. Participants must have taken an 8-week ceramics course at the Phipps within the last 3 years or seek special permission from the Studio Manager; no instruction will be provided during this 8-week session. Includes 10 hours of studio access per week, one 25lb bag of clay, access to pottery wheels, clay tools, glazes and kiln firing. Studio participants must sign up for timeslots on a weekly basis.

Class size is limited to 10.

Beginner Pottery on the Wheel

Tuesdays, April 1– May 20, 6:30 – 8:30 p.m. [WAITLIST]
Age 14+
Fee: $289

This class will cover the basics of throwing on the potter’s wheel and working with clay. Topics covered will include throwing cylinders and bowls, trimming and finished pieces, adding handles, and glazing. Complete beginners and those with some experience are welcome to learn and develop their skills. Students will also receive 8 hours of studio access per week to practice outside of class.

Class size is limited to 9.

Register 2/3

Intermediate/Advanced Pottery on the Wheel

Thursdays, April 3– May 22, 6:30 – 8:30 p.m.
Age 14+
Fee: $289

Level up your pottery skills! Intermediate and Advanced students will learn more complex techniques to create forms such as plates, lidded jars, pitchers, etc. as well as developing and honing skills and craftsmanship. Throughout the session there will be demonstrations from the instructor as well as one-on-one time to work through your own ideas. Students will also receive 8 hours of studio access per week to work on projects independently.

Class size is limited to 9.

Art Studio One
Age 6 – 14
Fee: $32

Come celebrate springtime and make your own fairy or frog house for your garden! Kids will create, build and decorate their houses, experimenting with clay techniques for construction and design. The instructor will coat with a clear glaze and kiln fire the projects which will be available for pick up 2-3 weeks after the class.

Class is limited to 10.

Watercolor Foundations

Wednesdays, April 9 – May 14, 6:30 – 8:30 p.m. [WAITLIST]
Tuesdays, April 8 – May 13, 1 – 3 p.m. [WAITLIST]
Art Studio One
Fee: $160
Instructor: John Grancorvitz

Join us for this fun, beginner-friendly six-week watercolor class! Whether you are a complete novice, looking to brush-up your skills or continuing a new practice, this class offers an introduction to the basics of color mixing, composition, and brush techniques. Explore your creativity as you build and practice new skills! Material list will be emailed prior to the first class.

Class size is limited to 10.

Art for Kids: Sense of Spring Workshop

Saturday, April 12, 10 a.m. – 12 p.m.
Ages 7 – 12
Fee: $32
Instructor: Petra Guenthner-Johnson

Dreaming of Spring? Shake off the winter blues and join us for this paper collage workshop where we will use a playful mix of magazine images, paper scraps, color and doodling to awaken our senses to the sights, sounds and feelings of spring. We will craft beautiful collages that celebrate the season. No experience is needed, and all materials are provided.

Class limited to 12.

Creative Together: Adult/Child Woven Flower Workshop

Saturday, May 3, 10 a.m. – 12 p.m.
Ages 8+ with adult
Fee: $32 per person – adult and child MUST register as separate students
Instructor: Petra Guenthner-Johnson

Young artists (ages 8+) and their adults will learn the basics of circular weaving to create their very own, joyful woven flowers! Circular weaving is a flexible and expressive way to create woven artwork. It’s a perfect introduction for anyone who’s ever been interested in weaving. No experience is needed, and all materials are provided.

Class limited to 14.

Mother’s Day Clay workshop

Saturday, May 10, 11 – 1 p.m.
Saturday, May 10, 2 – 4 p.m.
Art Studio One
All ages
Fee: $32 per person

Bring mom and create with clay together! Learn basic slab and pinch building techniques to make your own special cup, bowl, or planter pot. During the workshop, you will both make and decorate your individual projects. Instructors will glaze and fire your pieces to be picked up approximately 2-3 weeks after the class date.

Class limited to 16.

Thursday Morning Painters

Thursdays, 10 a.m.-12 p.m.
Art Studio One
Ages 18+
Fee: no fee

Looking for a group of art friends that will help inspire you to keep painting and developing your talents? The Thursday Morning Painters (TMP) invite you to join their welcoming group in the beautiful second-floor studio at the Phipps Center for the Arts.

This informal gathering provides dedicated time and space to paint, develop your talents, and connect with fellow artists. Each session concludes with a completely optional critique, offering helpful advice and encouragement from peers. While participation is free, the group shows appreciation for use of the studio by maintaining an annual Phipps membership!

Figure Drawing

Spring: 4/13, 4/27*, 5/4, 5/18*. 1-3 p.m.

*single pose session

Ages 18+
Fee: $10, pre-registration required

Phipps Figure Drawing provides visual artists the opportunity to work independently and achieve self-improvement in a safe environment on a relaxing Sunday afternoon. Enjoy life drawing with fellow artists from the St. Croix River Valley and across the Twin Cities. We employ professional models. Sessions are facilitated by a Phipps Visual Arts Council member. Artists must bring their own supplies and an easel, if they have one. The Phipps has six easels available on a first-come basis.
